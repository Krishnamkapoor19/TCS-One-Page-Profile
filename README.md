## Company Overview

Tata Consultancy Services is the flagship company of the Tata Group and a global leader in IT services, consulting, and business solutions. With over 50 years of experience, TCS partners with the world's largest businesses in their digital transformation journeys, offering a consulting-led, cognitive-powered, integrated portfolio of business, technology, and engineering services.

**Workforce:** 6,07,979 permanent employees globally

---

## Financial Performance Summary

### Revenue & Growth Trajectory

TCS has demonstrated consistent revenue expansion over the past five years, growing from ₹1,64,177 crores in FY21 to ₹2,55,324 crores in FY25, representing a compound annual growth rate of approximately 12%.

| Fiscal Year | Total Sales (₹ Cr) | YoY Growth | Net Profit Margin |
|-------------|---------------------|------------|-------------------|
| FY21 | 1,64,177 | 5% | 20% |
| FY22 | 1,91,754 | 17% | 20% |
| FY23 | 2,25,458 | 18% | 19% |
| FY24 | 2,40,893 | 7% | 19% |
| FY25 | 2,55,324 | 6% | 19% |

**Key Observations:**
- Peak growth period in FY22-FY23 driven by post-pandemic digital transformation demand
- Growth moderation in FY24-FY25 reflecting global macro headwinds and IT spending rationalization
- Stable net profit margins maintained around 19-20% despite revenue scaling

## Profitability & Margin Analysis

TCS maintains industry-leading profitability metrics with stable operating leverage:

**Margin Progression (FY21 → FY25):**
- Gross Profit Margin: 43% → 38% (compression reflecting wage inflation and competitive pressures)
- EBITDA Margin: 28% → 26% (controlled decline demonstrating operational discipline)
- EBIT Margin: 26% → 24%
- Net Profit Margin: Stable at 19-20%

The margin compression over the period reflects strategic investments in talent, infrastructure, and capability building, while maintaining sector-leading profitability levels.

## Investment Perspective

**Strengths:**
- Market-leading position in global IT services with strong Tata Group parentage
- Exceptional capital efficiency metrics (ROE >50%, ROCE >65%)
- Consistent free cash flow generation enabling strong dividend payouts
- Strategic pivot toward AI and generative technologies positioning for next growth cycle
- Diversified client base across industries and geographies

**Considerations:**
- Growth moderation reflecting IT spending normalization post-pandemic boom
- Margin compression pressures from wage inflation and competitive intensity
- Valuation premium to broader market requires sustained execution
- Workforce optimization signals near-term demand uncertainty

**Valuation View:**
At 27x P/E, TCS trades at a premium to broader markets but below its historical range. The valuation reflects quality business fundamentals and market leadership, balanced against near-term growth concerns. For long-term investors, current levels may offer reasonable entry point given franchise value and structural positioning in digital transformation trends.
